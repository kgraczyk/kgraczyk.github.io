--- title: "Polarization asymmetries in neutrino scattering off nucleon" excerpt: "
The main goal of the project was to study the information content of the polarization asymmetries in: quasielastic (QE) charged current (CC) neutrino (antineutrino) neutron (proton) scattering [Phys.Rev.D 101 (2020) 7, 073002](https://journals.aps.org/prd/abstract/10.1103/PhysRevD.101.073002), and [Acta Phys.Polon.B 50 (2019) 1771](https://www.actaphys.uj.edu.pl/index_n.php?I=R&V=50&N=11#1771); weak (CC) pion production (SPP) in the neutrino (antineutrino) - nucleon scattering, [Phys.Rev.D 97 (2018) 1, 013001](https://journals.aps.org/prd/abstract/10.1103/PhysRevD.97.013001), [Phys.Rev.D 99 (2019) 5, 053002](https://journals.aps.org/prd/abstract/10.1103/PhysRevD.99.053002), [Phys.Rev.D 104 (2021) 3, 033005](https://journals.aps.org/prd/abstract/10.1103/PhysRevD.104.033005), and [Acta Phys.Polon.B 50 (2019) 1771](https://www.actaphys.uj.edu.pl/index_n.php?I=R&V=50&N=11#1771). The project has been done together with the Beata Kowal. Target spin asymmetry and double and triple spin asymmetries have been studied for the first time for the QE scattering. We showed that double spin asymmetries and triple spin asymmetry contain information about the axial content of the nucleon and are perfect observables for the measurement of the axial form factor of the nucleon.

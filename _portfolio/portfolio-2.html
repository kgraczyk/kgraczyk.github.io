--- title: "Electromagnetic and weak structure of the nucleon studied within Bayesian neural network methods" excerpt: "We adapted and further developed the Bayesian neural network (BNN) approach to investigate the electroweak structure of the nucleon. A key advantage of this method is its reduced sensitivity to specific model assumptions, leading to more robust and reliable analyses. This research resulted in a new parametrization of the proton and neutron electromagnetic form factors (JHEP 09, 053 (2010)) and an analogous study of the axial form factor (Phys. Rev. C 99 (2019), 025204). The latter demonstrated that neutrino–deuteron scattering data are insufficient to constrain non-dipole corrections. We also investigated the two-photon exchange effect (Phys. Rev. C 84, 034314; J. Phys. G 42, 034019) and compared BNN-based results with quantum field-theoretical calculations (Phys. Rev. C 88, 065205). In a further study, we applied a Bayesian objective method to determine the proton charge radius (Phys. Rev. C 90, 054334). Beyond the scientific publications, the project also led to the development of a dedicated BNN C++ library, written from scratch by K. Graczyk and C. Juszczak.

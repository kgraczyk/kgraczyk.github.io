--- title: "Deep learning and uncertainty estimation in bacterial colony analysis" excerpt: |

This project was carried out within the Mozart programme funded by the Wrocław Centre for Academic Cooperation (WCA) and developed in collaboration with the Wrocław-based IT company NeuroSYS. It focused on applying deep learning and statistical uncertainty estimation to intelligent models designed for counting and classifying bacterial colonies in Petri dishes.

The project built on my experience with artificial neural networks and statistical methods, previously developed in the analysis of scattering data. The main objective was to propose a reliable framework for estimating the uncertainty of predictions produced by deep-learning models used in biomedical image analysis. Particular attention was devoted to U-Net and U2-Net architectures, widely used for image segmentation and object detection tasks.

The main outcome of the project was the development of modifications to the U2-Net architecture that significantly improved counting precision, even in images containing a large number and a high diversity of objects. These results led to a publication in Scientific Reports and demonstrated that combining modern deep-learning approaches with robust statistical methods can substantially improve the reliability and practical value of automated image-analysis systems.
